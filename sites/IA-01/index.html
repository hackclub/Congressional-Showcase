About Me

Hi, I'm Kento Sugiyama. I'm interested in hydrology, geospatial computing, and building frameworks that make technical water-science work easier to understand and use.

CoFIS grew out of my interest in client-side hydrological analysis and from seeing how important flood information is nowadays. I worked with Professor Ibrahim Demir and Dr. Carlos Erazo Ramirez at the Hydroinformatics Lab at Tulane University.

The App

What CoFIS Does

CoFIS integrates federal hydrologic and geospatial datasets into a single web system for scenario-based flood inundation mapping and impact analysis. Users can move from national watershed views into nested HUC boundaries, inspect individual reaches, and generate flood scenarios using observed or forecast streamflow information.

The platform combines National Water Model data, National Water Prediction Service forecasts, Office of Water Prediction flood inundation products, NHDPlus hydrography, watershed boundaries, USGS stations, and National Structure Inventory data.

CoFIS uses WebAssembly, Web Workers, and IndexedDB to run flood-processing steps directly in the browser, caching data locally and carrying out the entire analysis workflow on the client side instead of depending on server-side computation.

Watersheds

Navigate from HUC-2 down to HUC-12, move through nested watershed boundaries, and inspect individual reaches in one interface.

Flood Inundation

Generate flood scenarios from observed conditions and forecast streamflow data directly in the browser-based workflow.

Impact

Connect flood extents to structure exposure and damage assessment.
