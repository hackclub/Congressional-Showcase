About me — Arnav Shenoy

A

Hi! I'm Arnav Shenoy, a high school senior (incoming CS+Stats major @ UIUC and Comp Neuro/Bio Researcher @ CMU, UCLA) passionate about using AI and ML to solve real healthcare problems. My App Challenge project initially started as a question — why do millions lose their eyesight to diseases that are detectable early? And so in order to answer my own question I built this app Oculare AI, a mobile app that combines a custom-trained neural network, a mobile frontend, and an AI chatbot into one accessible tool.

The Platform

Eye care intelligence, in your pocket.

Oculare lets anyone upload or capture a retinal image and receive an instant AI diagnosis — complete with disease probabilities, a plain-language summary, and a confidence rating. No clinic required for a first look.

Beyond detection, the app connects users to a curated database of 600+ ophthalmologists across all 50 states, and includes Visionary AI — a Gemini-powered chatbot that answers eye-health questions with context from your own scan history.

Tech Stack

How it's built

A full-stack mobile system — neural network inference on a Python backend, real-time cloud sync, and a cross-platform React Native frontend.

The AI works by feeding your retinal photo into a model called EfficientNetB4 — think of it as a highly trained visual expert that has studied thousands of real eye scans to learn exactly what Diabetic Retinopathy, Glaucoma, and Cataracts look like. Before analysis, the image goes through several enhancement steps (sharpening contrast, reducing noise, highlighting blood vessels) so the model sees the clearest possible version of your eye. It then outputs a probability for each disease, telling you not just what it detected, but how confident it is.
